# CRM Walkthrough Testing Guide (Realtor)

This guide walks through a real-world scenario. Focus on:
- How natural things feel
- What’s confusing
- What’s missing
- What you like or don’t like

---

## Scenario

A homeowner (Sarah Johnson) reaches out for a roof inspection.

You will:
- Create the lead
- Track tasks
- Create a job
- Upload files
- Watch how activity updates

---

## Phase 1 — Create a Lead

Go to **Leads → Create Lead**

Use:
- Sarah Johnson
- sarah@example.com
- 555-111-2222
- Notes: Roof inspection inquiry

### Think about:
- Was this easy?
- Did you know what to enter?
- Did anything feel missing?

---

## Phase 2 — Explore the Lead Page

Open the lead.

### Think about:
- Does this feel like a “contact hub”?
- Is anything confusing or unnecessary?
- Does it feel too empty or just right?

---

## Phase 3 — Add a Task

Create a task:
- Call Sarah
- Due tomorrow

### Think about:
- Was it easy to create?
- Did it feel natural?
- Did it feel connected to the lead?

---

## Phase 4 — Upload a File

Upload any file to the lead.

### Think about:
- Does it clearly belong to the lead?
- Can you preview/open it easily?
- Does it feel organized?

---

## Phase 5 — Create a Job

Go to **Jobs → Create Job**

- Roof inspection for Sarah
- 123 Main St

### Think about:
- Does this feel different from a lead?
- Does it feel like “work” vs “contact”?

---

## Phase 6 — Use the Job Page

On the job page:

- Look at the pipeline
- Create a task
- Upload a file

### Think about:
- Does this feel like a workspace?
- Is the pipeline intuitive?
- Does everything feel connected?

---

## Phase 7 — Activity & Updates

Look at the **Activity section**.

### Think about:
- Does it help you understand what happened?
- Is it too long or easy to scan?
- Do the groupings (Today, Yesterday, etc.) make sense?

---

## Phase 8 — Tasks Page

Go to **Tasks**

### Think about:
- Can you tell what belongs to what?
- Is it easy to navigate?
- Does it feel useful or overwhelming?

---

## Phase 9 — Estimates, sharing, and field data (build plan Phase 9 chunks)

Use a job that already has (or create) an **estimate** with at least one line item.

### 9.1 — PDF and share link

On the **estimate detail** page:

- Click **Download PDF** and confirm a PDF opens or downloads.
- Click **Copy share link** and paste the URL in a private browser window (or send it to a test inbox).

### Think about:
- Is the PDF clear enough to send to a homeowner?
- Was copying the link obvious?

### 9.2 — Client response (public page)

On the **shared estimate** page (no login):

- Open **View PDF** from the public page.
- Choose **Accept**, **Request revision**, or **Decline** (optional note).
- Back in the CRM on the same estimate, confirm **Client response** and status updated.

### Think about:
- Could a client complete this without calling you?
- Is the wording neutral and professional?

### 9.3 — Proposals (scope)

For MVP, the **estimate PDF + notes** stands in for a separate “proposal” document. No extra UI is required unless you later want branded templates.

### 9.4 — Measurements

On the **job** page, open **Measurements**:

- Add a row (e.g. label “Main roof”, value, unit “sq ft”).
- Edit and delete a row.

### Think about:
- Do measurements feel tied to the job in a useful way?

### 9.5 — ABC pricing sample

Go to **Integrations → ABC Supply** and click **Sample pricing lookup** (requires ABC env configuration).

### Think about:
- Does the response give you confidence the integration is wired, or is the error message clear when not configured?

---

## Phase 10 — Open a Task

Open both tasks.

### Think about:
- Is the context clear (lead vs job)?
- Does everything feel connected?
- Is anything missing?
